SUMaC Preparation

A proof-centered exploration of mathematical thinking, structure, and communication. This sequence emphasizes reasoning, clarity, and intellectual honesty over technique.
Week 0 — Orientation & Mathematical Mindset
Orientation, expectations, and how mathematicians think.
What proof-based math is (and is not); how SUMaC values explanations; reading, writing, and reflecting on proofs; growth mindset and intellectual honesty.
Week 1 — Proof Foundations
Foundations of logical structure and proof-writing.
Definitions and assumptions; direct proof, contrapositive, contradiction, and induction; writing complete, readable arguments; reasoning as a transferable skill.
Week 2 — Number Theory & Structure
Number theory foundations: structure, divisibility, and modular reasoning.
Divisibility, parity, and modular reasoning; translating English to algebra; congruence classes; impossibility proofs using structure; pigeonhole principle (intro).
Week 3 — Counting & Discrete Structures
Counting arguments and reasoning about finite structures.
Counting without formulas; case analysis; arrangement and selection arguments; deeper use of the pigeonhole principle; avoiding overcounting.
Week 4 — Invariants & Constraints
What must remain unchanged.
Invariants and monovariants; parity arguments; constraint-based reasoning; impossibility proofs; game- and process-based analysis.
Week 5 — Functional & Recursive Thinking
Rules, transformations, and recursive structure.
Functional equations; recursive definitions; iteration and self-reference; structural substitution; solving families of solutions.
Week 6 — Advanced Proof Strategies
Coming soon
Multi-layered proofs; strategic choice of method; tight logical sequencing; reading, critiquing, and polishing competition-level solutions.
Week 7 — Synthesis & Mathematical Communication
Coming soon
Integrating ideas across weeks; effective representations; elegant solution writing; explaining mathematics clearly to others; reflective synthesis.
Week 8 — Conclusion & Reflection
Coming soon
Program-wide reflection; growth as a mathematical thinker; preparing for future proof-based coursework and mathematical exploration.
